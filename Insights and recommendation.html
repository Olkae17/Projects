Older patients (>60) tend to be more satisfied than other age groups, possibly because hospitals pay more attention to them.
6
7
Patients who stayed for more than 20 days are more satisfied compared to those who spent less time. Those who stayed 10-20 days are the least satisfied, potentially because they were discharged before fully recovering.
8
9
Patients who do not adhere to their medications seem more satisfied than those who do, possibly due to fewer interactions with healthcare providers or lower expectations.
10
11
Patients sent to nursing or rehabilitation facilities are more satisfied than those sent home, possibly because those sent home were not fully healthy or confident in their recovery.
12
13
Follow-up appointments do not significantly increase patient satisfaction rates, possibly due to individual differences or ineffective follow-up practices.

15
Gender does not significantly impact care satisfaction, indicating the hospital does not discriminate based on gender.

Recommendations
24
25
Apply the same level of care and attention given to older patients (>60) to other age groups to enhance satisfaction across all demographics.
26
27
Ensure patients are fully healthy before discharge by conducting necessary tests and, when necessary, extending their hospital stays. Avoid premature discharges, even if requested by patients.
28
29
Educate and communicate with patients about the importance of medication adherence while addressing potential barriers to adherence.
30
31
Ensure patients are confident in their recovery before discharging them to go home, and consider expanding support for those sent home.
32
33
Train and inform the follow-up team to ensure follow-up appointments are meaningful and tailored to individual patient needs. Improve the process to make patients feel cared for during follow-ups.
34
35
Continue training staff to maintain unbiased care regardless of gender and monitor for any signs of gender-based bias in the healthcare facility.
36
37
Hire more specialized doctors and nurses in various areas to further improve patient care quality and satisfaction, especially for patients with chronic illnesses like diabetes.

Develop specific care programs for patients with comorbidities to ensure they receive the attention and support they need, improving their satisfaction rates.
